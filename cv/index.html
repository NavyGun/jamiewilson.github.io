I am ignorant.

I know, that’s not something you’d expect to see at the top of a CV. But wearing that ignorance on my sleeve is the biggest reason for the success I’ve had as a designer.

I’m quick to say it because I’m eager to start asking questions—to start learning something new and solving the next puzzle. I never want to be the person who has all the answers. I want to be the guy that questions everything.

A little more about me:

  • I need to be working on difficult problems. It makes me feel alive. If I’m not building something, I get grumpy.
  • I want to apply my branding skills to digital products because I think branding is most effective when it empowers a user to accomplish something, rather than just telling a story.
  • I’m passionate about motion and animation and how it can overcome the shortcomings of touch-screens and inform and modify a user’s behavior.
  • I desire a tight process that focuses on quantifiable results and goals—and allows for iteration based on predefined sources of feedback.
  • I’m very interested in behavioral psychology and how habits are formed. I especially enjoy BJ Fogg’s work and his “tiny habits”.
  • It’s hard for me to understand how some people don’t own a dog, I’m obsessed with black pepper, and I drink high-quality coffee to justify how much of it I consume.
